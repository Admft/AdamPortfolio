In-Person Free-Fall Device Investigation – June 2026
I supported an in-person store investigation into free-falling rail devices after a firmware update, using VManager data, physical rail inspections, and power profiler readings to isolate root causes across multiple affected rails. The investigation confirmed that many “bad battery” alerts were caused by field conditions rather than simple battery failure, including loose or improperly seated displays, dirty/stuck tags, dusty base-deck rails, high-current bad displays, and one faulty rail controller.
Key impact:
* Identified multiple high-current displays that were draining rails, including cases where removing a single bad display dropped current from 5.16 mA to 14–20 µA and from ~2.17 mA to very low microamp levels.
* Found that several rails recovered after reseating loose or partially inserted tags, with one rail dropping from 7–12 mA back to ~50 µA once the display was fully installed.
* Diagnosed a faulty rail controller on Z001-6VKA, where current stayed at 242 µA even after removing displays, then dropped to a healthy ~20 µA after replacing the controller.
* Proved that high insert counts and dusty/dirty base-deck conditions were contributing to false battery issues and unstable rail behavior.
* Helped narrow the issue from a broad “bad battery/free-fall” problem into actionable hardware, installation, and maintenance findings.
Brag-sheet version:
Conducted a hands-on field investigation of free-falling rail devices, combining VManager diagnostics, physical inspection, and power profiler testing to identify root causes behind bad battery alerts and abnormal current draw. Isolated issues to improperly seated displays, dirty/stuck tags, dusty rails, bad rail displays, and a faulty rail controller. Several fixes immediately reduced current consumption from milliamp-level drain to healthy microamp levels, helping turn a broad field issue into specific corrective actions for hardware replacement, display reseating, and rail maintenance.

TopStockAutoWebScript is a Python 3 automation tool for VManager TopStock in VUSION / Walmart WMPC, originally built for store uswm.1216 — TX:1216 Carrollton SUP, but configurable for other chains and stores. It uses Playwright sync API >= 1.40.0 to drive a real Chromium browser through launch_persistent_context, preserving Microsoft SSO login in a local .browser_profile so users only need to log in once with --login-only. The script automates the repetitive process of visiting roughly 90 topstock rails, selecting either the Topstock or Shelf camera, reading the “On demand image capture” counter, clicking the camera icon, and confirming the capture dialog until each rail reaches its quota, defaulting to 6 Topstock captures and 3 Shelf captures. To avoid the site’s cooldown when switching cameras too quickly on the same rail, it works in full alternating passes: all rails on Topstock, then all rails on Shelf, repeating until complete. It saves resumable progress to local JSON files, reads rail IDs from rail_ids.txt, and skips rails when the quota is already met, capture is in progress, the camera button is disabled, or the counter cannot be safely read. The main file is topstock_capture.py, with support files including requirements.txt, .gitignore, generated capture_progress*.json files, the generated .browser_profile, and saved VManager HTML snapshots used for selector inspection. It targets Windows 10/11 and PowerShell, stores data locally in plain text and JSON, and does not use a database, web server, Docker, CI pipeline, or automated test suite.
